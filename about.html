About

My name is Anas Morsi, and I’m a computer science student at Kettering University in my junior year. I was born in Flint, MI to Egyptian immigrant parents, and I was raised in Flint. As a child growing up I had interests in many things including sports, music, movies, but I had a special interest in technology specifically computers. My parents were never fond of how much time I spent using our desktop home computer, but in hindsight they’re happy they let me slide some nights to stay up exploring all the functions of our home computer. Being born to Egyptian parents I travel back to Egypt often to reconnect with family and friends and to keep up to date on my culture and heritage that has been so close to my heart. Ironically, going back to Egypt helped fuel my passion for web development and UX design as many of my family members work as engineers and computer engineers. I started off as student at the University of Michigan-Flint, but I knew that with Kettering just around the corner I would’ve been doing myself a disservice not transferring to their world class computer science program, and so I did just that. I have gained so much out of my experience in my first year, and I am eternally grateful for my co-op and the opportunities presented here at Kettering. After my experience with my start-up as a web developer and user experience designer I found I had a great interest in web development and UX design. I was able through Kettering to find a great co-op and really expand on my skill set. I’ve worked many jobs in my lifetime to pay bills and have money in my pocket, but I finally feel like I’ve started working for something more than money. It seemed only natural to choose a degree which interested me. After completing several web applications for clients, I realized that web development and UX design is the occupation I would like to follow on successful completion of my degree. My work experience taught me a lot about the computer industry especially the processes that are involved in designing and implementing a website. I also worked on the design and front end development for a very big and well established company and my final product went up live. Being able to work on big name projects while also being a student is a very valuable tool that my university offers, and it has helped me immensely as I progress into a full-time web developer and designer. Outside of school I enjoy sports, especially soccer and basketball. I also enjoy reading computing publications as I like to keep up to date with new technology. I also enjoy listening to music especially Arabic music, rap, and hip-hop. I also enjoy traveling the world and seeing the mix of cultures; I enjoy experiencing different ways of life and trying not to be a tourist, but trying to speak the local languages and observe their traditions. I think this gives me a better perspective on the countries I visit, and it also opens my mind to different environments and paces of life. My experiences traveling have also shown me different perspectives on where certain parts of the world are from a technological standpoint, which is valuable to my overall understating as to why I am pursuing the career that I am pursuing.
